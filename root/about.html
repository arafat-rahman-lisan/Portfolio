Bio

On a mission to bring tech education where it’s needed most, one line of code at a time.

I’m a Computer Science & Engineering student at American International University-Bangladesh (AIUB), building real systems and research with a purpose-driven mindset. I work across ASP.NET Core (C#), algorithms and data structures, databases, and machine learning, always focusing on clean engineering and practical outcomes.

Through Qubit Catchers, the organization I founded and lead as CEO, I’m creating pathways for rural learners to explore competitive programming, quantum computing, and modern software skills. My goal is simple: bridge the digital divide and help students turn curiosity into confidence, and confidence into capability.

I’ve built projects like CINEX, a full-stack movie ticket management system with seat holds, payments, admin management, and PDF invoices. Alongside development, I’m actively involved in research work spanning technology for women’s safety, smart fabrics and comfort innovation for Bangladesh’s RMG sector, and the real-world impact of AI tools in primary healthcare.

Beyond tech, I value leadership, clear communication, and community-building. I enjoy mentoring, organizing, and collaborating across teams. Fluent in Bangla, English, and Hindi, I connect across cultures and bring a calm, focused approach to every challenge I take on.
